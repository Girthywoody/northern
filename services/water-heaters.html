Tank or Tankless?

Two great ways to make hot water. Here's how they compare — and we'll help you pick the right fit.

Storage Tank

A storage tank keeps 40 to 60 gallons of water hot and ready, so it's there the moment you need it. Tanks cost less to install, suit most family homes, and pair easily with existing gas or electric setups. With annual maintenance, a quality Bradford White tank delivers 8 to 12 years of dependable service.

Tankless (On-Demand)

A tankless unit heats water only as it flows, so you get an endless supply and never pay to keep a tank warm overnight. Navien and Rinnai systems mount on the wall to free up floor space, run 15 to 20 years, and trim energy use — a smart upgrade for busy households and larger homes.
