Frequently Asked Questions

A tank water heater stores and continuously heats a reservoir of water (typically 40-60 gallons), providing a steady supply until the tank is depleted. A tankless (on-demand) water heater heats water only when you turn on the tap, offering unlimited hot water and lower energy bills since it is not keeping a tank warm around the clock. Tankless units are more compact but have a higher upfront cost, while tank models are more affordable to install. Our team can help you decide which option is best for your home and budget.
A conventional tank water heater typically lasts 8 to 12 years with proper maintenance. Tankless water heaters generally last 15 to 20 years. Signs your water heater may need replacing include rusty or discoloured water, unusual noises, leaks around the base, or inconsistent hot water. We recommend scheduling an inspection if your unit is over 10 years old.
